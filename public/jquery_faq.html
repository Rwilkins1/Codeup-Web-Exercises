Frequently Asked Questions

Who is the director of the National Park Service?
Jonathan B. Jarvis

What government agency oversees the National Park Service?
The Department of the Interior directly oversees the National Park Service.

How many employees are in the National Park Service?
Approximately 22,000

How old is the National Park System?
The first national park, Yellowstone National Park, was established in 1872.

What is the largest national park site?
The Wrangell-St. Elias National Park and Preserve.

What is the smallest national park site?
The Thaddeus Kosciuszko National Memorial.

How many people visit the national parks?
About 292,800,082.

How do I obtain a park pass?
By visiting your nearest park site.

Where can I find a park map?
You can find a park map on a specific park website.

What are concessions?
Concessionaires provide loding, transportation, food service, shops, and many other services.

Yellowstone National Park

  • Yellowstone is home to 40 major waterfalls
  • Yellowstone was America's first national park
  • It was declared a national park before its state was officially recognized.
  • It is home to approximately 300 active geysers

Carlsbad Caverns National Park

  • It is located in New Mexico
  • The biggest cavern is 3,800 feet long by 600 feet wide
  • The cavern is home to a pool that looks shallow, but is actually hundreds of feet deep.
  • It was discovered in the late 1800's

Glacier National Park

  • Glacier is home to 762 lakes.
  • 631 of these lakes are unnamed.
  • It is home to 277 documented species of birds
  • True to its name, the park contains 25 glaciers.
